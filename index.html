THEO HAND

Theo Hand

ME 193C - Upper Limb Prosthesis

Theohand is a prosthetic device that utilizes soft robotics to help our Project Partner Theo improve his mobility and way of life. Theo is a 3 year old boy born with only one-third of his right forearm. Because of Theo’s young age and lack of experience with prostheses, the user experience of the device was a huge priority.

The prosthetic functions by turning a knob that tightens the prosthetic into a flexible grip.The device also has LEDs and a speaker that signaled different lights or sounds as haptic feedback to convey that an object was firmly grasped.

Destroy the World in a Day Simulator

New Media 190 - Critical Practices

Destroy the World in a Day simulator is an interactive game inspired by 80’s arcade games and choose your own adventure games. This game is focused on educating individuals about sustainability through the lens of everyday life decisions.

Players go through a typical day making decisions about what to eat, how to dress, etc. and see how their decisions immediately impact the environment and the social , political , and economic entanglements of each repercussion. As each arcade buttons were pressed, the color of the neopixel ring each time indicated the health bar of the planet.

Past Projects

PLANT-E

Plant-E is an internet of things product designed to be a self-care watering system that can collect data that can be monitored on an android app. The design was motivated by student’s experience with constantly killing their plants due to busy schedules.

This product followed a human centered design process focusing on student’s needs,and how students interact mostly on their phones through apps and twitter. Features include an interactive android app and a live twitter read of current plant updates and a Wall-e and cute aesthetic for marketability.

WII-KART

The Wii-Kart was an interdisciplinary project that synthesized visual programming with data acquisition and mechanical design. It uses the myRIO microprocessor as the controller by reading and processing accelerometer data. The myRIO VI then sends motion and light controls to both the Wii-Kart and the computer’s graphical user interface. Wii-Kart is a wireless, battery-powered vehicle which moves on four omni-wheels.

This product took inspiration from glowing LED balls, and aims to create a real life Mario Kart controlled in a similar way as with a wii remote.

COOL FAN

Aimed to solve issues surrounding student’s complaints of lack of air conditioning in classrooms, cool fan is designed to provide a solution to issues encountered with existing alternatives such as hand-held fans and desk fans.

Cool fan is uses four bar mechanism with a dyad that aims to consistently point directly at the face of the user. This product is portable, attachable to different desks, adaptable to different fans, and quiet and non invasive. This product focused on a human-centered design process.

SKELETOIDS(LBNL)

As visual thinkers, molecular models are especially useful as educational tools in introducing the fundamental backbone and building blocks that ultimately construct complex molecular structure. This project aims to present an accurate translation of the hierarchal self assembly of cis-structured peptoid nanosheet and helix.

The models are built to self-assemble, which aims to illustrate and mimic self-assembly in nature. These models will provide more opportunities for interaction and exploration, which helps engage students and users.

I NEED

This product aims to create a platform for people to voice their needs and address the specific needs the UC Berkeley student body. Inspired by Candy Chang’s “I Wish this Was”, which gave the public of post-Katrina New Orleans ability to voice their opinion about how how they wished their city to be rebuilt. This product give people the ability to cast a symbolic vote as a litmus test of what was really needed among the UC Berkeley student body and foster a sense of collective empathy.
