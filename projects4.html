Skeletoids

Macroscopic Self Assembly Models
Summer 2018
Introduction

As visual thinkers, molecular models are especially useful as educational tools to show simple building blocks that ultimately construct a complex molecular structure. This project aimed to present an accurate translation of a cis-structured peptoid nanosheet and helix.The models are built to self-assemble, which aims to illustrate and mimic self-assembly in nature through natural intermolecular interactions into its most stable structure. These models will provide more opportunities for interaction and exploration, which helps engage more users in the process.

  • Size : ~ 2.5 in x 0.8 in x 2.5 in
  • Medium : digital fabrication
  • Type: Professional; Solo Project
    • My Role

    I worked on this project under the mentorhsip of Ron Zuckermann as a reserach intern for Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. My job was to and discover a way to design and prototype macroscopic models that would accurately depict the self assembly of peptoids in order to create an interactive educational experience. My design process entailed ideating models and mechanical formation , modeling on CAD software, creating unique key slot joints, 3-D printing, tolerancing , prototyping and iterating .

    The Problem

    Facilitate learning and exploration on how complex molecular structures are assembled such as peptoids.

    Why Models?
  • used as educational tools for many years
  • more efficient in helping translate the structural elements and spacial relationships better than computer generated graphics
  • dynamic representations
    • Research
    Understanding & translating the science to create an accurate representation

    In order to design an authentic product that would truly represent what was happening at the molecular level, I looked at ramachandran plots to see acceptable pairs of dihedral angles. Once the appropriate angleâ€™s range was obtained , the structures were modeled in a molecule visualization software, various angle pairs were tried out to get the most linear or straight helix and sheet within the acceptable range. Once the molecule was built, the peptoid was deconstructed to its fundamental backbone. This was done by creating nitrogen trace using vectors from each Nitrogen.

Slots can not be symmetirc about the x and y axis or be a squeeze fit joint.optimized the slot design to ensure other slots do not go into other slots. Facilitated self assembly with appropriate toleraces and drafts.

    (2) Unlike the nanosheet, the helix is only made up of one building block. Therefore there is only one unique reversible interconnection in the design. Slots can also not be symmetric about the x and y axis and can not be a squeeze fit joint.
