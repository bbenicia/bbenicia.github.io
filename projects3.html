Finding Cuby

Macroscopic Self-Assembly Cubes Through Imaging and Kinetics
Introduction

Finding Cuby is a cube that self-rotates from the momentum of the laser-cutted wooden cube, which includes a flywheel, a DC motor, a solenoid to stop the flywheel in order to provide impulse to flip the cube, and a Pixy camera setup to detect the color of the cube -- as well as the distance between the cube and the camera. Multiple cubys are aimed to self assemble once located through magnet attachment. The potential application can be used in assembling modular furniture.

My Role

In a team of four,consisting of all mechanical engineering students, I conceptualized the idea having worked on self assembly models. I worked on the entire design process, but my main responibilities included harge of the mechanical design of what encompasses the mechanism : flywheel , mount, motor brake system along with the programming of the motor brake actuation and industrial design of the exterior. I also specialized in rapid prototyping - 3D printing and laser cutting along with material selection. Each iteration brought a new technical challenge that helped me mainly learn the importance of tolerancing, material selection, fundamental physics , and attention to details that ultimately guide the mechanical design.

(1) Every element from screws,wires connections, motor used were needed to be designed to optimize our end goal of rotating our cube. Each iteration made our system more and more robust because of the attention to details. For instance, the placement of our brake depended on maximizing the moment arm from the corner of the cube under the geometric restrictions of our flywheel placement. For the flywheel design , the inertia was maximized by creating the largest radius possible while still fitting within the parameters of the enclosure and other components that needed to fit in the system. The material was also considered on mass for the minimum thickness and tolerancing for machining process.

(2) In terms of the mount ,I had to think about not only the most robust and optimal design to fit every component with tolerances but also the printing process that would allow me to achieve this. It was important to consider the time of material used for the 3D printer, print speed, printing position , and infill to optimize strength. All these components mattered, since a visibly perfect printed mount cracked with the high rpm.

(3) A solenoid pushes forward a hexagonial brass prism that forcefully stops the flywheel from spinning in order to trasfer as much rotational energy as possibly from the flywheel to the cube.
