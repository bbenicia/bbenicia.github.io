Whatever The Weather

A Weather Inspired Reflective Digital Musical Instrument
Bryanna Benicia | bryannabenicia2023@u.northwestern.edu
Northwestern University , COMP SCI 497 Digital Instrument Design Spring 2022
Introduction

Do you find yourself jumping to the beat of the music in the rain? Are rainy days the best days to reflect and listen to music?Do you add rain background music on top of the music you are listening? The weather influences our emotions and state of being.I wanted to highlight performance as expression and reflection through the vessel of music. For me , the weather , specifically rain has always inspired me and been a time for self meditation and introspection. It is the days I feel most in tune with myself and can focus on myself. This lends itself to prime musical creation and expression. Whatever the Weather is a weather inspired digital musical instrument that encourages users to embrace the weather and use it as inspiration to make new compositions. Simply put it is essentially a weather themed sequencer. Whether used as a purposeful introspective instrument that users can reflect and self mediate with , or something to do when stuck inside due to being rained in or snowed in, this digital musical instrument provides different modes of interactions. The user can control “temperature” or “different weather patterns”(thunder, rain) through hardware controls (buttons,pressure sensor, etc) that map onto different sonic results. Visual feedback is provided for the execution of these controls through LEDs.

Inspiration

Inspired by electronic musical artist Whatever the Weather's album of the same name and fascination and creative drive with rain. Whatever the Weather's album song tracks are named after varying degrees of temperature that create ambient textures , sonic glacial landscapes that iliict assocations with the varying degrees. Hearing the album inspired me and made me reflect on my own narratives with the weather.

Sound Design

All sounds in my instrument were created by me in abelton and input into max as samples. For the sound design , I wanted to create ambient textured twinkly sounds for light rain and deeper lower octave fuller sounds for the hard rain. Hard and small thudner would have a crashing, noisy , distorted sound of varrying degrees. The Max code is fairly simple ,mainly consisting of arduino max serial communication , and sampling processing.

Hardware Elements
  • 8 .5"x.5" buttons ; 4 buttons of chubbier depth than other 4
  • Potentiometer Slider
  • FSR sensor

  • Neopixel Led strip
    • Sound and Control Mapping

  • Pot Slider -- user can move the slider vertically, mapped from 0 degrees to 100 degrees which controls the overall volume of instrument
  • Buttons 1-4 -- Light Rain , buttons on left side of instruments will represent light rain controlled by 4 buttons and be sonically respresented by 4 twinkly notes ( AEBD????).
  • Buttons 5-8 -- Hard Rain , buttons on right side of instruments will represent hard rain controlled by 4 buttons and be sonically respresented by 4 deeper chord notes that is a lower octave than the light rain notes.
  • FSR Sensor, light pressure -- Small Thunder ,represent small thunder controlled by small pressure applied to fsr and be sonically respresented by a distorted high hat sound
  • FSR Sensor , hard pressure -- Hard Thunder ,represent hard thunder controlled by large pressure applied to fsr and be sonically respresented by a distorted cymbal sound

    • Physical Design: The Cloud Box

    The physical design of the box was very important. I wanted it to look like a cloud box , and try my best to have a high fielity prototype of what the final product would look like. I lasercut using white and clear acrylic. The clear acylic was used for the top and bottom of the box , while white acrylic was used for the sides to provide a nice contrast and give a more sleeker look. The transparent was to fit the aesthetic of the product , allow users to look inside , and give focus to the rgb lights. Looking down on the box is a large layerd clouds , that I lasercut, with an led matrix below it. I etched any words and symbols on , such as the thermometer temperatures


    Desiging Product Interactions

    I put alot of thought into the desiging the product interactions and how it would support the feelings I wanted the users to have and the way that would provide the best interactions. I thought alot about button placement and mapping and felt that there should not be distractions on the top face of the box where the cloud was held. The users would look down at the instrument and the focus of the instrument would be the cloud and rain falling down. This would be the focus of what the users would see and where they also get their visual feedback of their actions. I took inspiration from pinball machines and placed my buttons on the left and right sides of the box , therefore giving a more natural placement of the hands and also taking advantage of associations users would already have with pinballs and natural hand placments wrapping around the box. Also with pinball machines you are focused on seeing the top face and what magical things are happening just like with this instrument. The fsr sensor was placed on the front face , following along the natural hand curve needed to press the buttons.

    The inclusion of the leds was essential to the instrument. I wanted my instrument to be an expierence , to be a reflective tool and be a magical solitary expierence. I wanted it not only be a musical tool , but an expierence. The leds are a somewhat of a show to accomany the music and be mesmorized with BUT ALSO is functional in providing visual feedback when an action is created. Every "weather effect" has its own associated led effect and color. When you press any of the light rain or hard rain buttons it is mapped onto a led colum. Upon pressing you get the auditory feedback , the sound, and the visual feedback, the led lights up according to which button you pressed and every two seconds it goes down the colum to mimic rain as rain falls down from the cloud. Hard rain and light rain are similar with only differing in color of the leds. The tunder has a flashing sequence of leds with varrying colors.
